His mission: to boldly fetch where no dog has fetched before.

CAPTAIN'S LOG

USS BISCUIT · K-9 CORPS STARFLEET · CLASSIFIED: VERY GOOD BOY LEVEL

Stardate 2387.01 — Entry #001
🐾 First Contact with the Mailman Nebula

We've made it past the Mailman Nebula without incident. Cody detected the nebula from approximately 47 parsecs out — well before sensors registered anything. I have no scientific explanation for this. Chief Science Officer Spock says it may be related to Cody's "extraordinary olfactory processing." I say it's because Cody hates mailmen on a molecular level. Either way, we've marked the nebula on the charts as a zone of extreme barking.

— Capt. Bob, Commanding Officer, USS Biscuit
Stardate 2387.14 — Entry #002
🦴 The Bone Heist of Sector Seven

The Space Cats have stolen our emergency bone reserves again. Cody tracked them to a rogue asteroid and single-handedly retrieved 14 bones, 3 squeaky toys, and what appears to be an alien tennis ball of unknown origin. When asked how he located the stash, Cody licked my face and wagged his tail. I'm writing this down as "advanced tactical instinct." Starfleet will never know the difference. Also Cody is now wearing the alien tennis ball as a hat and refusing to take it off. It suits him.

— Capt. Bob
Stardate 2387.28 — Entry #003
🌌 Warp Speed Incident — The Squirrel Anomaly

Cody engaged the warp drive unprompted today. When I asked what caused the spontaneous jump to warp 9.7, the ship's AI informed me that long-range sensors had detected a "small, fast, furry object" in the Rigel system. This is the third time this month. Engineering has requested we install a "squirrel dampener" on the warp controls. Cody is unrepentant. He found the squirrel, he says. He always finds the squirrel. The squirrel escaped into a nebula. Cody has staked out the nebula exit for six hours. We are all waiting. Even Darth Whiskers seems curious.

— Capt. Bob, currently eating a sandwich in the command chair
Stardate 2387.42 — Entry #004
🚀 Cody Saves the Galaxy (Again, Somehow)

I must report that Commander Cody has once again saved this vessel, this crew, and arguably the entire known universe. Darth Whiskers had us cornered near the Black Hole of No Return (officially called HR-6819 but nobody uses that). The Space Cat fleet was closing in. Ammunition depleted. Shields at 12%. And then Cody picked up his tennis ball launcher, pressed his big fluffy face against the viewport, and barked so loudly at Darth Whiskers that the entire enemy fleet broke formation in a panic. Scientists are calling it a "sonic anomaly." I'm calling it a good boy being a very good boy. Commendation filed. Extra treats allocated. Gold star on the fridge.

— Capt. Bob, filing this from the couch with Cody asleep on my feet
